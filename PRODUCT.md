# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The BaliDoc clinic team (~10 people): admins (Bart, Sidney) coordinating everything, and staff (GM, doctors, marketing, ops — Fafa, Gabriel, Meilinda, dr. Bintang, dr. Karina, kak Adel/Ani/Asima) working their assigned tasks. Mixed technical comfort; several are non-native English speakers. Used daily on desktop (Chrome) and phones, often between patient-facing work — sessions are short and task-focused.

## Product Purpose

The app is named **Plendex**; **BaliDoc** is the clinic/company behind it ("Plendex by BaliDoc"). **Plendex** (by BaliDoc) is the clinic's self-hosted project management tool (an Asana replacement, HIPAA-minded: encrypted fields, audit logs). It exists so the team plans, assigns, and tracks all clinic/ops/marketing work in one private place. Success = every teammate checks their Dashboard/My Day daily and nothing falls through the cracks.

## Brand Personality

Warm, dependable, unfussy. Three words: **caring · clear · calm**. It should feel like a well-run clinic front desk: friendly ("Welcome back, Bart 👋"), never corporate-cold, never playful to the point of noise. Emotional goal: relief — "I can see what's on my plate."

## Anti-references

- Jira: dense enterprise chrome, config sprawl, jargon.
- Generic AI-generated SaaS: gradient heroes, glassmorphism, decorative motion.
- Old Asana as the team experienced it: cluttered boards, notification overload.
- Anything that looks like a developer tool — the audience is clinic staff.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Earned familiarity over novelty; standard affordances (Linear/Notion-class), no invented controls.
2. **One glance = one answer.** Every screen leads with what the user must act on (overdue, assigned, blocked). Severity is visible before reading.
3. **Red means BaliDoc or means act.** The brand red is spent on primary actions, selection, and alerts — never decoration. Everything else stays quiet neutral.
4. **Write like the front desk.** Plain verbs, sentence case, specific labels ("Post", "Attach file"), errors that say how to fix it. English simple enough for non-native speakers.
5. **Dense but never cramped.** Staff scan lists all day — favor compact rows and real information density over fat cards and dead space.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, visible focus rings on everything interactive, keyboard-reachable menus/dialogs, ≥44px touch targets on mobile. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Copy readable by non-native English speakers (short sentences, no idioms in UI chrome). Phones matter: the team genuinely uses the mobile drawer/board views.
